Cardre scorecard workflow

Build scorecards that can be explained, replayed, and reviewed.

Cardre guides a credit-risk modeller from local data to a governance-ready scorecard pack. The workflow keeps model development transparent: every population definition, sample window, split, bin, variable choice, score, and validation result is visible and auditable.

What Cardre gives the modeller

  • Local-first modellingPortfolio data stays on the user's machine.
  • Structured leakage controlOnly train data is used to build the scorecard; holdout samples are scored later.
  • Manual judgement where it mattersSegmentation, coarse classing, overrides, and inclusion decisions are recorded with reasons.
  • Governance evidence by defaultThe final export explains what happened and why.

The modelling journey

A fixed pathway keeps the first product simple while matching the professional scorecard workflow: build on train, then score and evaluate train/test/OOT consistently.

Build streamFit the scorecard using train only.
5

Fine class all variables

Create initial bins and calculate early WOE/IV diagnostics across candidate variables.

6

Cluster and select

Group correlated variables, then keep candidates using IV, stability, business admissibility, and judgement.

7

Coarse class manually

Merge bins, isolate missing and special codes, and record every override reason.

8

Fit scorecard

Calculate final WOE, fit logistic regression, and scale coefficients into scorecard points.

Scoring and evaluationApply the built scorecard without refitting.
9

Score train

Score the development sample for benchmark performance and overfit checks.

10

Score test

Apply the same bins and coefficients to the holdout sample.

11

Score OOT

Measure time-based stability using the out-of-time sample where available.

12

Compare evidence

Review gains, cutoff trade-offs, Gini, KS, calibration, PSI, fallbacks, and implementation parity.

Capabilities that matter

The product focuses on the parts of scorecard development that are hard to govern in notebooks and spreadsheets.

B

Manual binning

Inspect WOE and event-rate charts, merge sparse bins, isolate special codes, and require a reason for every judgement call.

V

Variable selection

Track why variables were included or excluded: IV, correlation cluster, stability, business inadmissibility, or analyst override.

L

Leakage prevention

The workflow separates scorecard building from holdout scoring so test and OOT samples cannot accidentally influence fitting.

R

Replayable runs

Change a binning decision or threshold and rerun only the affected downstream work while preserving previous evidence.
